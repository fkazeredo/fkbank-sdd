## Findings

See `.claude/runtime/SPEC-0018/qa-report.md` §Findings for full detail and repro commands.

1. **[BLOCKER, first pass]** Self-referencing redirects (`/login`, re-redirect to
   `/oauth2/authorize`) dropped the port through the nginx edge. **CONFIRMED CLOSED in the
   rework pass** — independently re-verified (exact original repro, full redirect-chain trace,
   green Playwright run), not merely trusting the builder's report. Fixed by `ff0ff97`:
   `proxy_set_header Host $http_host;` (was `$host`) plus `X-Forwarded-Host`/`X-Forwarded-Port`
   in `frontend/nginx.conf`, and `server.forward-headers-strategy: framework` in
   `backend/src/main/resources/application.yml`.
2. Investigated, resolved as not a defect: `ProductionSecretsGuard`'s apparent single-property
   coverage is correct given `application-prod.yml`'s defaults and `SeededLoginConfig`'s profile
   restriction.
3. **[NEW, rework pass — OBSERVATION, not blocking]** The Host-header fix, combined with
   `frontend/nginx.conf`'s unpinned `server_name _;`, lets an external caller forge its own
   `Host` header and poison the `/login`/`/oauth2/authorize` self-referencing redirect target
   (open-redirect-class). Does not affect the OIDC issuer, token issuance/validation, or
   default-deny — bounded to the redirect target only, and not exploitable via a normal browser
   click (requires a raw crafted request). Recommended fix: pin `server_name` in `nginx.conf` to
   the known origin instead of the catch-all `_`. Recorded for the owner to schedule; not
   returned as a blocking rework item.

## Verdict

**PASS_WITH_OBSERVATIONS** — see qa-report.md §Final verdict for full reasoning.
